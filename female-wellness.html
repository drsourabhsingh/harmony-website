Understanding PCOS / PCOD in Simple Words

PCOS is not just an ovary problem. It is usually a pattern involving hormones, metabolism, lifestyle, stress response and cycle regulation.

Why Periods Become Irregular

In many PCOS patterns, ovulation does not happen regularly. When ovulation is delayed, periods may also get delayed or become unpredictable. This is why only waiting for the next period may not solve the root pattern.

Why Acne, Hair Fall or Weight Gain Happens

Hormonal imbalance and insulin resistance can influence skin, hair, weight and energy levels. That is why a detailed history of diet, sleep, stress, digestion and activity becomes important.

Why Early Care Helps

When cycle disturbance, acne, weight changes or fertility concerns are addressed early, the treatment plan can become more structured. Early care also reduces unnecessary fear and confusion.
